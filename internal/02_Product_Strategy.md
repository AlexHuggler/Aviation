# SoloTrack - Product Strategy

## 1. Product Vision

SoloTrack aims to be the clearest, least intimidating digital logbook for student pilots. It is not trying to out-feature broad aviation platforms; it is trying to reduce confusion at the exact stage where pilots most need structure: logging flights correctly, staying current, understanding what still counts toward the checkride, and keeping momentum between lessons.

The strategy centers on one belief: student pilots benefit more from guided clarity than from raw flexibility. SoloTrack therefore combines logbook capture, compliance interpretation, progress visibility, system-level convenience, and export readiness in one focused iOS product.

---

## 2. Current Product Positioning

### Positioning Statement

SoloTrack is a local-first flight logbook built for student pilots, combining fast flight logging with real-time training progress, currency awareness, digital endorsements, App Shortcuts, widgets, and a live flight-session timer.

### What the Product Is Today

As of April 18, 2026, SoloTrack is no longer just a simple offline logbook. The current shipped product includes:

- Stage-aware onboarding and defaults for student-pilot workflows
- FAR 61.57 day, night, instrument, and flight-review tracking
- FAR 61.109 progress and checklist visibility
- Expanded training-detail capture for checkride-specific experience
- Digital CFI endorsements with lock/unlock behavior
- CSV import from multiple formats with duplicate review
- CSV and PDF export for backup, transfer, and checkride preparation
- Optional iCloud sync for flights and templates
- Smart notification nudges for high-value training moments
- iPad sidebar navigation in addition to the iPhone tab experience
- Home Screen widgets for currency, progress, and last-flight glanceability
- App Shortcuts / App Intents for quick logging, status checks, and timer control
- A single active flight-session timer with control-widget and Live Activity support

In other words, SoloTrack is now a multi-surface product. The main app remains the center of gravity, but widgets, App Shortcuts, the control widget, and the Live Activity extend the same student-logbook workflow beyond the foreground app.

The product’s strategic role is now broader: SoloTrack acts as the student’s operational record, progress mirror, readiness aid, and quick-access aviation dashboard across multiple system surfaces.

## 3. Core Value Proposition

### 3.1 For Student Pilots

SoloTrack reduces uncertainty in five places that matter most:

- **Logging**: flights can be entered quickly with smart defaults, templates, route reuse, quick entry, timer-prefill handoff, and ICAO assistance
- **Legality**: the dashboard, widgets, and Shortcuts make currency status visible without separate calculators
- **Progress**: FAR requirements and checklist items are translated into plain-language progress surfaces
- **Readiness**: signed flights, import/export, and printable PDF output make the logbook easier to review and share
- **Convenience**: widgets, App Shortcuts, control-widget access, and a Live Activity timer reduce friction between flying and logging

### 3.2 For CFIs and Checkride Prep

Even though SoloTrack is student-owned, it also supports adjacent instructor and checkride workflows:

- CFIs can sign directly in-app and lock the record
- Students can preserve endorsement context with remarks and signature imagery
- PDF export provides a cleaner review artifact than raw spreadsheet data
- Training-detail fields make checkride-relevant experience easier to verify

### 3.3 For Migration and Retention

Import/export and shared surfaces reduce adoption risk:

- Import lowers the barrier to switching from paper or other logbooks
- Export keeps the user from feeling trapped
- Optional iCloud sync improves backup confidence without forcing account creation
- Widgets and App Shortcuts increase day-to-day usefulness without requiring a full app session

## 4. Target Audience

### 4.1 Primary Audience: Student Pilots

SoloTrack is optimized for the journey from pre-solo through checkride prep.

| Stage | Product Need | Current SoloTrack Support |
|-------|--------------|---------------------------|
| **Pre-Solo** | Fast dual logging, currency visibility, early habit formation | Stage-aware defaults, dashboard empty states, CFI signature capture, quick logging |
| **Post-Solo** | Solo/XC progress visibility, recurring route reuse, backfill speed | Templates, quick entry, progress nudge, import/export, widgets |
| **Checkride Prep** | Requirement gap closure, endorsements, clean records | Detailed checklist, PDF export, training-detail fields, signed-flight locking, timer-to-entry workflow |

### 4.2 Secondary Audience: CFIs in the Moment

SoloTrack does not target instructors as standalone users yet, but it is intentionally usable during instructor interactions:

- endorsement capture
- CFI certificate storage on templates and flights
- locked records after signing
- clean exports for review

### 4.3 Tertiary Audience: Pilots Maintaining Recency

The app can also support pilots beyond initial training through:

- day/night currency
- instrument currency
- flight review tracking
- local-first logging with optional sync
- shortcut and widget status checks

However, the product language and primary UX remain student-first.

## 5. Key Differentiators

### 5.1 Student-Journey-Aware Product Design

Most logbooks assume the user already knows what matters. SoloTrack instead adapts defaults, empty states, and guidance based on where the student is in training.

### 5.2 Regulatory Translation, Not Just Data Storage

SoloTrack does not stop at collecting entries. It interprets them into:

- day/night passenger currency
- instrument recency
- flight review recency
- top-level PPL requirement progress
- checklist-style training gaps

This turns the logbook into a decision-support tool.

### 5.3 Local-First With Multi-Surface Convenience

The current product avoids an account-first posture while still being convenient:

- daily use works offline
- no sign-in is required
- users can stay local-only if they want
- users can opt into iCloud sync when they want backup and multi-device continuity
- widgets, Shortcuts, a control widget, and a Live Activity extend the product into system surfaces without changing that local-first core

### 5.4 Migration and Exit Friendliness

Many niche apps trap users with weak import/export. SoloTrack now competes better here by shipping:

- multi-format CSV import with preview
- duplicate flagging before commit
- CSV export for raw portability
- PDF export for printable review

### 5.5 Logbook-to-Action Workflow

SoloTrack increasingly helps the student act, not just record:

- recommendation-driven next milestone logging
- status queries through Shortcuts
- flight-session timer handoff into Add Flight
- dashboard and widget surfaces that reduce time-to-answer for “What should I do next?”

SoloTrack should not try to beat the big incumbents on breadth. It should win by being:

- clearer for early-stage pilots
- lighter-weight to adopt
- easier to trust during training
- more useful from the system surfaces students already touch throughout the day
- less bloated than all-purpose aviation suites

## 7. Strategic Principles

The current product suggests five durable principles:

1. **Reduce ambiguity, not just taps**  
   Features should answer “Am I current?” and “What am I missing?” faster than they ask users to interpret raw data.

2. **Keep the workflow reversible**  
   Import/export and local-first usage should remain strong so users never feel trapped.

3. **Prefer contextual intelligence over generic reminders**  
   Notifications, widgets, and Shortcuts should stay high-signal rather than becoming clutter.

4. **Invest where student pilots repeat work**  
   Templates, quick entry, route reuse, timer handoff, and defaults matter because student logging is repetitive.

5. **Add surface area only when it improves readiness**  
   New features should make the student more prepared, not just add aviation-adjacent complexity.

## 9. Technical Strategy Alignment

The current implementation supports the product strategy in useful ways:

| Product Need | Current Architectural Support |
|--------------|-------------------------------|
| Student-specific defaults | `OnboardingManager`, `TrainingStage`, personalized empty states |
| Compliance interpretation | `CurrencyManager`, `ProgressTracker` |
| Repeated flight entry | `FlightTemplate`, quick entry, route reuse, timer prefills |
| Migration and portability | `ImportParser`, `CSVExporter`, `PDFExporter` |
| Local-first + cloud option | `SyncSettings`, CloudKit-backed SwiftData mode selection |
| Cross-surface convenience | `AppGroup`, `SharedModelContainer`, widgets, App Intents, Live Activity |
| Contextual alerts | `NotificationEvaluator`, `NotificationService`, lifecycle coordinator |

The architecture is still compact enough to move quickly while already supporting more sophisticated behavior than the original foreground-app-only logbook concept.

## 10. Risks and Watchouts

Current strategic risks to manage:

- **Scope creep**: the app is adding useful surfaces quickly and should avoid becoming a generic aviation suite
- **Documentation drift**: app, widget, Shortcut, QA, and marketing materials can easily fall out of sync
- **Cloud expectation mismatch**: the UI/docs must stay explicit about what does and does not sync
- **Notification and automation trust**: widgets, Shortcuts, and alerts are only wins if they stay reliable and understandable
- **Feature discoverability**: templates, import, PDF export, timer handoff, and system surfaces add value only if users find them

---

## 11. Strategic Summary

SoloTrack’s strongest position is no longer “simple offline logbook.” Its stronger and more defensible position is:

**the student-pilot logbook that turns flight records into clarity across the app and the system surfaces around it.**

That means future work should continue to reinforce:

- guidance over complexity
- readiness over raw feature count
- reversible ownership over lock-in
- local-first trust with optional cloud convenience
- fast access from widgets, Shortcuts, and timer surfaces when the student is between lessons, on the ramp, or heading home
