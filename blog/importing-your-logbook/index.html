How to import your logbook into SoloTrack

Moving from paper, ForeFlight, LogTen, or MyFlightBook? Here is how to bring your flights across cleanly using CSV — without creating duplicates.

Published May 29, 2026 · SoloTrack Team

Home›Blog›Importing your logbook

Switching logbooks should not mean retyping years of flights. SoloTrack imports from CSV and ships with presets for the most common formats, plus duplicate detection so a re-import does not double your hours. Here is the whole process.

Step 1 — Export a CSV from your current logbook

SoloTrack reads CSV files. Get one out of wherever your flights live now:

  • ForeFlight: export your logbook to a CSV/file from the Logbook section and save it where your iPhone or iPad can reach it (Files, iCloud Drive, or email to yourself).
  • LogTen: export your entries to CSV.
  • MyFlightBook: download your flights as CSV.
  • A spreadsheet: save your sheet as CSV (one flight per row, a header row on top).
  • Paper: there is nothing to export — skip to Step 3 and use Quick Entry templates to backfill, or type your past flights into a spreadsheet first and export that as CSV.

Step 2 — Open Import in SoloTrack

In the app, open Import / Export and choose your CSV file. SoloTrack supports Auto Detect plus presets for SoloTrack CSV, ForeFlight-style, LogTen-style, MyFlightBook-style, and Generic CSV. Start with Auto Detect; if a column lands in the wrong place, pick the matching preset.

Step 3 — Map columns and review

SoloTrack parses your file and shows how it read each column — date, route, Hobbs/Tach, day and night landings, and category toggles like Solo, Dual Received, Cross-Country, and Simulated Instrument. Review the preview before you commit so the categories that feed your FAR 61.109 progress and 61.57 currency are correct.

Step 4 — Let duplicate detection do its job

SoloTrack flags likely duplicates so importing the same file twice — or importing a CSV that overlaps flights you already entered — does not inflate your totals. If you ever re-export and re-import after fixing something, the duplicate check has your back.

Step 5 — Verify your totals

After importing, glance at your dashboard. Do total time, dual, solo, cross-country, night, and instrument look right? If a category reads low, it usually means a column in the source CSV was not mapped — re-run the import with the right preset, and duplicate detection will keep things clean.

A note on accuracy: your imported totals are only as good as the source data. SoloTrack is a logbook tool, not a regulator — confirm your hours and requirements with your CFI and the current FARs.

Already importing? Here is the payoff

Once your history is in, SoloTrack keeps your currency and PPL progress current automatically, captures CFI signatures digitally, and exports a clean CSV (22 columns) or a printable PDF whenever you need to hand records to a CFI or DPE. For first-time setup, see Getting started with SoloTrack.
