Pilot Currency Calculator (FAR 61.57)

Am I current to carry passengers? Enter your recent takeoffs, landings, and instrument events to estimate your day, night, and instrument currency.

This is an estimate, not a regulatory determination. SoloTrack is a logbook tool, not a regulator. Currency also depends on category, class, type, and night/full-stop conditions that this tool does not verify. Always confirm currency with your CFI and the current Federal Aviation Regulations.

Day currency — FAR 61.57(a)

3 takeoffs and 3 landings in the preceding 90 days. Add the dates of your most recent landings.

Night currency — FAR 61.57(b)

3 takeoffs and 3 landings to a full stop at night, in the preceding 90 days. Add the dates of your most recent night full-stop landings.

Instrument currency — FAR 61.57(c)

6 approaches, plus holding and intercepting/tracking, in the preceding 6 calendar months. Add the dates of your instrument approaches.

How currency works under FAR 61.57

Currency is what keeps you legal to carry passengers (or, for instrument, to act as PIC under IFR) — it is separate from the FAR 61.109 experience you build to earn the certificate. The three rules:

  • Day (61.57a): 3 takeoffs and landings in the preceding 90 days, same category/class/type.
  • Night (61.57b): 3 takeoffs and full-stop landings at night, preceding 90 days.
  • Instrument (61.57c): 6 approaches, holding, and intercepting/tracking in the preceding 6 calendar months.

SoloTrack does this automatically
