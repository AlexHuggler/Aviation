What SoloTrack is

SoloTrack is a local-first iOS flight logbook for private-pilot students. It is meant to turn logbook entries into practical training visibility: what has been flown, what is still missing, and what record is ready for a CFI or DPE to review.

Student-first logbook

Built for pre-solo through checkride prep, with stage-aware onboarding and defaults for common student workflows.

Training progress, not just rows

Tracks FAR 61.109 progress, FAR 61.57 day/night/instrument currency, and the FAR 61.56 flight-review window from the flights a student logs.

Local-first by design

No SoloTrack account is required. Daily logging works on device, with optional iCloud sync for flights and templates.

Who it is for

SoloTrack is primarily for active PPL students. CFIs can use it in the moment when reviewing or signing a student's record, but there is no instructor dashboard or multi-student school console today.

Pre-solo students

Start clean habits early: quick dual-flight entries, currency visibility, and a dashboard that shows what matters next.

Solo and XC builders

Reuse common routes, log cross-country details, and keep progress visible instead of waiting for a spreadsheet check.

Checkride-prep students and CFIs

Review requirement gaps, capture CFI signatures, lock endorsed records, and export a cleaner PDF for review.

Real workflows it supports

The current product is narrow on purpose. These are the workflows SoloTrack is designed to support today.

Log a lesson quicklyUse templates, route fields, Hobbs/Tach, landings, category toggles, remarks, and CFI number fields.
2
Track PPL progressSee FAR 61.109 buckets such as total, solo, dual, cross-country, night, and instrument progress.
3
Check currency statusMonitor day, night, instrument, and flight-review recency from logged flight data.
4
Use timer-to-entryStart a flight session, keep it visible with iOS surfaces, and prefill a logbook entry afterward.
5
Capture CFI signaturesUse PencilKit signature capture and lock endorsed records until a signature is voided.
6
Import existing dataBring in CSV logbook data with duplicate detection and review before committing entries.
7
Export for reviewCreate CSV or PDF exports so the student can share cleaner records with an instructor or examiner.
8
Keep it accessibleUse widgets, App Shortcuts, and a Live Activity to reduce friction around logging and status checks.

How it could complement FLY8MA

FLY8MA teaches students what they need to know. SoloTrack helps them track what they have actually flown and what still needs attention.

The fit

For a student watching ground school, checkride prep, or training videos, SoloTrack can be a companion record: after the lesson, the student logs what happened and sees whether that flight moved them closer to a requirement or kept them current.

The ask

The smallest useful review is simple: one FLY8MA instructor tries the no-paywall TestFlight build for about 10 minutes and says whether it is worth showing students, not yet ready, or missing something obvious.

What SoloTrack is not

SoloTrack should be judged as a student logbook and training tracker, not as a full aviation platform. It is not an EFB, not ground school, and not a substitute for a CFI.

Not an EFB

It does not provide charts, weather briefings, flight planning, dispatch, or in-flight navigation.

Not ground school

It does not teach maneuvers, regulations, oral prep, or written-test material.

Not a substitute for a CFI

It is not a CFI, DPE, FAA source, or regulatory decision-maker. Students should confirm requirements and readiness with their instructor and current FARs.

Current product limits

  • iOS only.
  • No multi-student instructor dashboard today.
  • No direct ForeFlight, Garmin, FAA, or school system integration today.
  • Calculations are convenience features based on entered data, not FAA or DPE approval.
